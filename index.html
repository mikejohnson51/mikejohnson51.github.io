About

My name is J. Michael Johnson, but everyone calls me Mike. I am a PhD candidate in Geography at the University of California, Santa Barbara working with Keith C. Clarke.

Before coming to UCSB, I recieved my B.S in Geography & Anthropology from Cal Poly where I received minors in GIS for Agriculture, Water Science, Statistics, Economics, and Environmental Studies.

Nowadays, I develop software to help researchers more easily find, format and interact with environmental data and use this software to study how geospatial data is utilized and represented in environmental models.

Due to the challenge of estimating model inputs at regional scales, I am also involved in aspects of big data discovery, processing, and analysis to better understand environmental phenomena including hydraulic estimates and hydrologic impacts of disturbances (like fire).

I am also involved in applications of discrete, process-based, and agent-based modeling in an effort to better understand how chnages in human behavior, land use and climate will impact hydrologic and water resources systems in the western United States.

If you are interested, or would like to work together, please see my current work, publications, and software or reach out at jmj00@ucsb.edu
